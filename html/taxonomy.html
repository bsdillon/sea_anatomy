Reading a Cladogram

When biologists disagree on the evolution of creatures, they use a cladogram to show the hypothetical relationship between them. Here we have two conflicting cladograms showing relationships between current species A, B, C, and D with older and possibly extinct parent species 1, 2, and 3. Answer these questions about the cladograms on the left and right. If you can't tell, just say so.


  1. According to the left cladogram, is A more closely related to C or D? What about the right cladogram?
  2. On the left, what is the most recent ancestor of C and D? On the right?
  3. On the left, did A separate from B before or after C separated from D? On the right?
  4. On the left, is D more recently evolved than C? On the right?

Even when biologists agree on which creatures are more closely related to each other, the time of separation is another factor to consider. A dendrogram shows the time of separation in a way cladograms do not. Contrast the cladogram with the dendrogram and answer the questions. If you can't tell, just say so.


  1. According to the cladogram, is A more closely related to C or D? What about the dendrogram?
  2. On the left, what is the most recent ancestor of C and D? On the right?
  3. On the left, did A separate from B before or after C separated from D? On the right?
  4. On the left, is D more recently evolved than C? On the right?

Finally, when all the evidence is in, a phylogram can show changes on each branch since a common ancestor. Contrast the phylogram with the dendrogram and answer the questions. If you can't tell, just say so.


  1. According to the phylogram, is A more closely related to C or D? What about the dendrogram?

  3. On the left, did A separate from B before or after C separated from D? On the right?

Using Taxonomy
